Data Privacy Notice
Republic Act No. 10173 — Data Privacy Act of 2012

The Department of Transportation (DOTR) is committed to protecting the privacy and security of personal data in accordance with Republic Act No. 10173 (Data Privacy Act of 2012) and its Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR), as well as the guidelines issued by the National Privacy Commission (NPC).

1. Personal Information Collected

The DOTR-LMS collects and processes the following personal information:

  • Full name, employee ID, and position
  • Division and office assignment
  • Email address and contact number
  • Date of hire and employment status
  • Salary grade
  • Profile photograph (optional)
  • Training enrolment, progress, and completion records
  • Assessment scores and competency levels
  • Individual Development Plan (IDP) activities
  • System access logs (IP address, login/logout timestamps)

2. Purpose of Processing

  • Manage employee learning and development activities
  • Track training completion and issue digital certificates
  • Conduct competency assessments and gap analysis
  • Generate reports for CSC PRIME-HRM compliance
  • Administer the Individual Development Plan (IDP) process
  • Maintain audit trails for system security and accountability
  • Integrate with the DOTR Human Resource Information System (HRIS)

3. Legal Basis for Processing

  • Employment relationship — processing is necessary for the performance of duties under Civil Service Commission rules
  • Legal obligation — compliance with CSC PRIME-HRM, COA regulations, and DICT standards
  • Consent — for optional data such as profile photographs

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

Your personal data may be shared with:

  • DOTR Human Resource Information System (HRIS) for synchronisation
  • Civil Service Commission (CSC) for PRIME-HRM compliance reporting
  • Commission on Audit (COA) for audit purposes
  • Authorised DOTR personnel (HR Officers, Supervisors, System Administrators)

We do not sell, trade, or otherwise transfer your personal information to outside parties except as described above.

5. Data Retention Schedule

Data Type Retention Period Legal Basis
Training completion records 5 years CSC PRIME-HRM
System access / audit logs 3 years COA regulations
Assessment attempts (non-passing) 1 year Operational necessity
Employee profile data Duration of employment + 5 years Civil Service rules
Erasure request records 1 year after completion NPC compliance proof

6. Your Rights as a Data Subject

  • Right to be Informed — be notified of what data is collected and how it is used
  • Right to Access — request a copy of your personal data held by DOTR-LMS
  • Right to Rectification — request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data via your profile page
  • Right to Erasure / Blocking — request anonymisation of your personal data (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Right to Object — object to the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances
  • Right to Data Portability — request your data in a structured, commonly used format
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint — file a complaint with the National Privacy Commission (NPC) at privacy.gov.ph

To exercise your rights, log in to DOTR-LMS and visit your Profile page, or contact the Data Protection Officer below.

7. Security Measures

  • Encrypted HTTPS communication (TLS/SSL)
  • Password hashing (PBKDF2) with complexity requirements
  • Role-based access control (RBAC) with six permission levels
  • Session timeout after 8 hours of inactivity
  • Audit logging of all access and data modification events
  • Server-side file upload validation (max 5 MB)
  • HTTP security headers (HSTS, XSS protection, Content-Type sniffing prevention)
