What is a Competency Framework?

A competency framework defines the knowledge, skills, and attitude/behavior needed for all personnel in the organization. Each individual has its own set of competencies needed to perform the job effectively. Defining each competency is necessary for the success in the organization, hence, helps to achieve the following:

  • Ensure that your people demonstrate sufficient expertise.
  • Recruit and select new staff more effectively.
  • Evaluate performance more effectively.
  • Identify skill and competency gaps more efficiently.
  • Provide more customized and inclusive learning and professional development.
  • Plan sufficiently for succession.
  • Make change management processes work more efficiently.

Three Critical Principles

1. Involve the people who are doing the work.

These frameworks should not be solely developed by the HR people. Instead, a collaboration of the Services, Directors, Division Chiefs/Unit Heads/Office-In-Charge and other relevant individuals per office helps identify and validate the needed competencies per position.

2. Communication.

A process by which information can be shared to open up and gauge curiosity over a framework, development or performance issues — sharing its importance and objectives would align everybody's understanding to embrace changes or improvement for easy implementation.

3. Use relevant competencies.

The identification of relevant competencies would bring forth a new leap of understanding towards the attainment of better organizational performance. Demonstrate a clear view of the overall objectives towards the attainment of the Office's mandates, thrust and directions.

Creating a competency framework is an effective method to assess, maintain, and monitor the knowledge, skills, and attitude/behavior of people in the Organization. The framework allows you to measure current competency levels to make sure your staff members have the expertise needed to add value to the Office's thrust. It also helps leaders/managers to be decisive in the recruitment process, retention and succession strategies of the organization/office, and identify the needs and address competency gaps per individual.
